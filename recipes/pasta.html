Bolognese Pasta

Home


Bolognese pasta is a hearty and comforting Italian classic made with a slow-cooked meat and tomato sauce. The blend of minced beef, vegetables, wine, and herbs creates a rich flavor that pairs perfectly with pasta — traditionally tagliatelle, although it works great with many shapes. It’s an ideal dish for batch cooking, as the sauce tastes even better the next day.


Ingredients

  • For the sauce
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 1 carrot, finely chopped
    • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
    • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 500 g ground beef (or beef/pork mix)
    • 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 150 ml red wine (optional but recommended)
    • 200 ml beef or vegetable broth
    • Salt and black pepper
    • 1 tsp dried oregano or thyme
    • 1 bay leaf

  • For the pasta
    • 350–400 g pasta (tagliatelle, spaghetti, rigatoni…)
    • Grated Parmesan cheese to serve

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for 5–7 minutes until soft.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
  3. Add the ground meat and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon.
  4. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer for 2–3 minutes to reduce.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, broth, oregano/thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover slightly, and simmer for 30–45 minutes (the longer, the better). Stir occasionally.
  7. Cook the pasta according to package directions.
  8. Drain the pasta.
  9. Combine the pasta with the sauce or serve the sauce on top.
  10. Finish with grated Parmesan cheese and a drizzle of olive oil.
